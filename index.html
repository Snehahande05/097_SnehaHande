Rani Lakshmi Bai

The Warrior Queen of Jhansi

About Her

Rani Lakshmi Bai, also known as the Queen of Jhansi, was a fearless leader and a symbol of resistance against British colonial rule in India. Born on 19 November 1828 as Manikarnika Tambe, she became queen after marrying the Maharaja of Jhansi. She played a key role in the Indian Rebellion of 1857 and died fighting on the battlefield in 1858, becoming a legend of Indian history.

Her Legacy

  • Led Jhansis defense against the British during the 1857 rebellion.
  • Trained women warriors and fought with unmatched bravery.
  • Inspired generations of freedom fighters in India.
  • Remembered for her courage, patriotism, and leadership.
